TRANSPORTING PEOPLE ACROSS MARTIAN CITIES

fast & SIMPLE point-to-point transport services on mars

The Mars Transporter (MPORT) would be transport people to anywhere on Mars (particularly Martian Cities) in under 35 minutes. To be clear, this is point-to-point transportation on Mars. MPORT will never leave Mars' sphere of influence. This spacecraft, similar to SpaceX's Starship, uses CH4 (Methane) as fuel and Liquid Oxygen as propellant. Methane is used as it is more powerful than Hydrogen (which can be easily produced from water) and can be made on the surface of Mars via the Sabatier Process. Since Mars' atmosphere is very thin (practically non-existent) and its gravity is much weaker than Earth's, this spacecraft only requires 1 stage with 3 engines to launch 100+ people to another destination on Mars. At Sea Level on Mars, the engine nozzles are slightly "overexpanded". This means that the nozzle ending is so wide that the hot expanding gases that come out of the nozzle are at a low enough pressure when they exit the nozzle so that the atmosphere starts to "crush" the gases. This creates a beautiful "mach diamond" effect, but it also decreases engine efficiency at sea level, however, it increases engine efficiency in a vacuum (outer space) when operating. The RS-25 Engine used on NASA's space shuttle and SLS is similar.

MPORT would become the primary method of long-distance transportation on Mars, and, assuming currency still exists, a seat would be very inexpensive, as only the propellant would cost money. Every physical part of this spacecraft is re-usable. That includes the heat shield tiles (thermal protection system) used during atmospheric re-entry, the deployable landing legs, the three engines, and every other part you can think of. It's like an airplane, once it lands, it only needs to be refueled to fly again. No replacing parts, or anything else like that. This is why a seat on MPORT would be extremely cheap. Additionally, by the time MPORT would actually carry humans, every part of every system on the spacecraft would have been tested thousands of times, and the actual spacecraft would have been launched hundreds of times in a row without a single failure. This would minimize the chances of any system failing while carrying humans, as any issues would have been fixed during the testing campaigns, making MPORT more reliable than modern-day commercial Earth airplanes.

HUNDREDS OF TRIPS AROUND MARS EVERY SINGLE DAY OF THE MONTH

The Mars Transporter vehicle will be capable of launching and landing hundreds of times every single day, every month, every year, nonstop. OCCASIONALLY, mport will need to undergo inspections to check for and repair any possible damage or weaknesses which have developed. MPORT WOULD ALLOW ANYONE, TO TRAVEL TO ANYWHERE ON MARS, IN UNDER 35 MINUTES.
